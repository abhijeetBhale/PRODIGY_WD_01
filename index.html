Ford GT

High-performance endurance racing car

History

The Ford GT40 is a legendary racing car born from a rivalry with Ferrari. Developed to conquer the grueling 24 Hours of Le Mans, this American-built machine defied expectations. With its low, aerodynamic profile and powerful V8 engine, the GT40 dominated the race for four consecutive years, solidifying Ford's place in motorsport history and becoming an iconic symbol of engineering prowess and American determination.

The Birth of a Legend

The Ford GT40 is a testament to American engineering and determination. Its origins lie in a fierce rivalry with Ferrari, who dominated the 24 Hours of Le Mans race. Determined to break the Italian stronghold, Ford assembled a team of talented engineers and drivers. Their goal was ambitious: to build a car capable of not only winning Le Mans but dominating it.

Engineering Excellence

The GT40 was a marvel of engineering. Its low, aerodynamic profile was designed to cut through the air with minimal resistance. Under the hood lay a powerful V8 engine, providing the necessary horsepower to compete with the best. The car's chassis was built for endurance, capable of withstanding the grueling demands of the 24-hour race. The result was a machine that was as beautiful as it was formidable.

Conquering Le Mans

In 1966, Ford achieved the impossible. The GT40 not only won Le Mans but dominated the podium, taking the first three positions. This incredible feat marked the beginning of an era. Over the next four years, Ford secured an unprecedented four consecutive victories, cementing the GT40's place in motorsport history. This triumph was a watershed moment for the American automotive industry, proving that they could compete and win against the best in the world.
